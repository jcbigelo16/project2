Biography

Ocean Vuong was born in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam on a rice farm on October 14, 1988. He briefly lived in a Philippines refugee camp at the age of two. They eventually migrated to Hartford, Connecticut where Vuong became the first person in his family to read. Vuong later enrolled at Brooklyn College of the City University of New York, where he studied 19th-century English literature under poet and novelist Ben Lerner, and received his B.A. in English. Vuong received his M.F.A. in poetry from New York University. He has since produced two notable works including “Night Sky with Exit Wounds” and “On Earth We’re Briefly Gorgeous.” Ocean Vuong has received 14 awards including notable prizes such as The Pushcart Prize, Whitling Award for Poetry, and the T.S. Eliot Prize. He is also a MacArthur Fellow.

Night Sky with Exit Wounds

Night Sky with Exit Wounds is the debut full-length poetry collection of Vietnamese-American poet Ocean Vuong. This collection is an exploration of themes of life and death, family issues, nationhood and violence. The collection consists of 34 raw and emotive poems that draw upon Vuong’s need for survival in literal and figurative foreign lands.

On Earth We're Briefly Gorgeous

“On Earth We’re Briefly Gorgeous” follows a letter written by the main protagonist Little Dog to his illiterate mother. The novel unfolds in a series of vignettes through memories that touched Little Dog. These vignettes include working in a field as a teenager, finding his sexuality, being raised by his single mother and confronting the violence and aftermath of the Vietnam War. The setting takes place in Hartford, Connecticut and Vietnam. Symbols and motifs used in the book include Tiger Woods, monarch butterflies and a white dress. Other literary devices that are prominent in the book include metaphors, similes, irony and imagery.
